\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
This class represents a simple particle. 

A simple particle in our api has mass, velocity and position.

Both quantities can change.

The mass and the velocity are related by the definition of classical momentum $ \vec{p} = m*\vec{v} $.

The energy is given by $ \frac{p^{2}}{2*m} $ and in case the mass is zero the energy will be also zero, as well as the momentum, as well the velocity. When the momentum of the particle is zero it does not interact with any other particle, but it still can be moved to some position and it also can be given a mass.

\begin{DoxyNote}{Note}
If you have to work with particles which has a a fixed mass like an electron for example, then derive from \mbox{\hyperlink{classphysapi_1_1_p_simple_mass_particle_base}{P\+Simple\+Mass\+Particle\+Base}} your own class. 
\end{DoxyNote}
